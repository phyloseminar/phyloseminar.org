The current theme is pandemic analysis, and this the third talk in a series of 5 talks on that topic.

Today's speaker is Dr. Andrew Holbrook.

Andrew received his Ph.D. in Statistics from UC Irvine in 2018, working on the intersection between differential geometry and applied Bayesian inference.
He then did a postdoc with Marc Suchard at UCLA, where is is now an assistant professor of Biostatistics.
His research interests include computational statistics, Bayesian hierarchical modeling, viral epidemiology and brain imaging.
Welcome, Andrew, and thank you for participating!
