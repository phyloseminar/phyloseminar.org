Today's speaker is Chain-ing Liu.

Qianying did her PhD with Daihai He at Hong Kong Polytechnic University and then worked with Aaron A. King and Edward L. Ionides as a Data Science Fellow at University of Michigan in Ann Arbor. 

In general, Qianying is interested in developing theoretical and computational frameworks to address problems in infectious disease evolution and epidemiology.

Qianying is currently a postdoctoral research associate in the Theoretical Biology and Biophysics group at Los Alamos National Laboratory, working with Ethan O. Romero-Severson, Thomas Leitner, and Carmen Molina-Paris.
