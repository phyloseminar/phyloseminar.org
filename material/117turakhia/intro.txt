Today's speaker is Dr. Yatish Turakhia.

Yatish has been an assistant professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) at UC San Diego (UCSD) since July 2021. He works at the intersection of computer architecture and computational biology. In particular, he is interested in developing algorithms and domain-specific hardware accelerators that enable faster and cheaper progress in biology and medicine. Prior to UCSD, he was previously a postdoctoral scholar at the Genomics Institute at UC Santa Cruz and received his PhD student in from Stanford University. His work has been conferred with the best paper award at ASPLOS, IEEE Micro Top picks and NVIDIA graduate fellowship.

Welcome, Yatish, and thank you for participating!
