Today's speaker is Cedric Chauve.

Cedric obtained a PhD in theoretical computer science (enumerative combinatorics) from University of Bordeaux in 2000. He was an assistant professor in the department of computer science at University of Quebec in Montreal (UQAM) from 2001 to 2006, then moved in the department of mathematics at Simon Fraser University, where he is now a professor and the chair of the department; in 2021 Cedric was on leave to work for the biotech company Canexia Health. Cedric's research interests are in the development of algorithms for comparative genomics and phylogenomics, with a strong focus on genome evolution, microbial genomics and cancer genomics.

Welcome, Cedric, and thank you for participating!
